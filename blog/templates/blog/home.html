Why do you want your money in stock?

  1. Stock performs better than bond or bank CD in fighting inflation.
  2. Unlike real estate, which can take months and years to appreciate in value, you can trade stocks much easier and in a shorter period.
  3. With shorter turnover time in stock, you can take your earning and reinvest that money in more undervalued stocks and watch your money grow!

Even though you have your money in stocks, what can you do to help yourself minimize the risks associated with stocks? After all, many people invest in stock outside their normal career so they have little time to dedicate to evaluate the value of a stock. If you decided to invest in a stock, it is preferably to hold it for long term.

Why invest in stock for long term? Below are some of the benefits of investing for long term:

  1. Most investors lack the time and resources to trade like a day trader. We know your time is precious so with our method you pick a few stock to invest in and check back in a few weeks or months.
  2. Invest for longer term (i.e. more than a year) reduces your taxable portion so you get to keep more of your earnings!
  3. It has been demonstrated that long term (more than a year) investment has higher yield than short term. Many stocks also pay dividend, which guarantees yield in the long term even when stock price depreciates.
  4. Take the panic out of trading stocks. Stocks can rise and fall greatly in a short period of time, but it is the behavior of a stock over the long run that behaves more predictably. The stocks I picked will very likely follow an upward trend over the long run.
  5. Take the hassle out of moving your money from one stock to the next.

How much money do I need to start investing in stocks? Is there a high cost of entry?

One benefit associated with stocks is that there are many stocks offered at different price ranges. With that said, if you only just started to invest, it is suggested that you invest only with amount that you are comfortable with. However, it is important that you start early to take advantage of compound interest.
